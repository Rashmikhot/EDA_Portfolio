Analysis on
Neglected Tropical Disease Onchocerciasis

Neglected Tropical Diseases affect millions of poor people, mostly in remote and rural areas. These diseases cause pain, disability, and even death, but they are often ignored. With more support and awareness, many of them can be treated or even eliminated.

Brief Information About The Portfolio

I’m Rashmi Khot, a student of Computer Science and Engineering at KLE Technological University, Hubli. This website is about my project for the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) course in my 4th semester. In this project, we worked with data about Reported number of individuals treated for onchocerciasis disease. You’ll find information here about what we studied, how we prepared the data, what we found from the analysis, and the main things we learned.

Course Project

Our Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) project was a team effort where we worked together to find useful insights from a dataset. The goal was to better understand the topic and explore how our findings could help in real-world situations. We used Python for all the data work, as it has many helpful libraries and is easy to use.

Throughout the project, we received constant support and guidance from our professor, P. G. Sunitha Hiremath ma’am. She motivated us to improve each part of our work. We followed the KDD (Knowledge Discovery in Databases) method, which is a popular step-by-step process in data mining. It includes cleaning the data, combining different sources, changing the data into useful formats, selecting key parts, and finding patterns.

Importance of EDA Course Project

The Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) course project helped us understand how to check the quality of data, find patterns, and deal with unusual values. We learned important steps like cleaning data, choosing the right features, and making guesses (hypotheses) to build better models. EDA also showed us how to use graphs and charts to choose the right methods and explain results clearly. This project improved our thinking skills, helped us understand the subject better, and taught us how to solve problems using data. Overall, it gave us useful tools to make better decisions by finding important information in data.
