Empathy

WHO A blind man, due to onchocerciasis, is being guided by his grandson in Nigeria

WHO Team in Yemen

  • A Silent, Chronic Burden: Onchocerciasis is a long lasting parasitic infection affecting skin and eyes, causing severe itching, disfigurement, and gradual blindness deeply affecting quality of life in impoverished rural areas
  • Loss of Vital Independence: As eyesight fades, so does autonomy patients in remote communities, lacking rehabilitation services, become heavily dependent on others .
  • Widespread Social Stigma: The physical symptoms—itching, "lizard skin," nodules lead to isolation, reduced marriage prospects (especially for women), and sometimes are treated as curses
  • Intergenerational Impact: adults lose productivity, children often daughters are pulled from school to help at home. Nutrition, education, and future opportunities suffer .
  • Mental Health Toll: Though overlooked, onchocerciasis related epilepsy, stigma, and grief severely affect mental wellbeing patients are sometimes shunned or blamed
  • Entrenched Poverty: This is a disease of the poorest: it traps families in economic hardship. Land near rivers (key habitats for blackflies) becomes unusable out of fear, reducing community sustainability
  • Empowerment Through Community: partners promote community directed mass drug administration (CDTI): distributing ivermectin empowers locals especially women—as key distributors, building trust and ownership
  • Validation Through Elimination: WHO recently verified elimination in Niger (Jan 2025) a message of hope that concerted, empathetic efforts can free communities from this blinding, stigmatizing illness
