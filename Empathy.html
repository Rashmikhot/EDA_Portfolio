WHO A blind man, due to onchocerciasis, is being guided by his grandson in Nigeria

WHO Team in Yemen

  • Onchocerciasis is considered to be a neglected tropical disease with significant medical and socioeconomic impacts. The potential costs are enormous, as the disease often leads to chronic skin conditions, visual impairment, and even permanent blindness, which are typically progressive and irreversible. The care and support of individuals affected by onchocerciasis is both time-consuming and financially demanding.
  • Studies conducted in endemic regions of Africa and parts of Latin America estimate billions of dollars in lost productivity and healthcare-related costs annually. However, there is a lack of comprehensive data on the economic burden and social impact of onchocerciasis in the Indian context. In affected communities, families often take over caregiving responsibilities and assist with daily activities, thereby masking the true burden of the disease.
