Problem Description

Problem Statement :

To analyze global treatment trends for onchocerciasis from 2005 to 2023 using publicly available datasets from the World Health Organization (WHO), with a focus on identifying regional and national patterns and evaluating progress toward the WHO's 2030 NTD elimination goals. To predict the treatment count till 2029.

Objectives

To eliminate onchocerciasis (river blindness) as a public health problem through sustained mass drug administration (MDA) of ivermectin, effective vector control, and strengthened surveillance, with the ultimate goal of interruption of transmission and verification of elimination in all endemic

  • 1. Interruption of transmission in at least 80% of endemic countries by 2030.
  • 2. Elimination as a public health problem in affected regions.
  • 3. Scaling up treatment coverage using community-directed strategies.
  • 4. Monitoring and evaluation to verify when treatment can be safely stopped.
  • 5. Strengthening national health systems to integrate and sustain elimination efforts.
