Data Description

Neglected Tropical Disease (NTD) data includes information about diseases that mostly affect poor communities in tropical areas. It tracks who is infected, where the disease is found, and how treatments are working. This data helps in planning health programs, controlling disease spread, and supporting global health goals like SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-Being).

Dataset Name : Reported number of individuals treated for onchocerciasis

Dataset Link: https://www.who.int/health-topics/onchocerciasis#tab=tab_1

Data Description :

  • Country-level data (ISO-3 codes) for 38 endemic nations.
  • Annual counts from 2005–2023, with the latest year flagged.
  • 681 Tuples x 34 Attributes.(Before Preprocessing).
  • three WHO regions: Africa (30 countries), Americas (6), Eastern Mediterranean (2).
  • Value field shows number treated per country/year.
  • Enables trend analysis, cross-country comparisons, and gap identification toward 2030 elimination

Key data from Africa, the Americas, and Eastern Mediterian to highlight global progress and regional challenges in eliminating river blindness.

Onchocerciasis affects 99% of cases in Africa.

Over 218 million people needed treatment in 2022.

MDA (ivermectin) ongoing in 29 countries

America

Elimination achieved in 6 endemic countries.

Last cases in Venezuela and Brazil

Focus on post-treatment surveillance

No new infections

Major public health success

Onchocerciasis is endemic in Sudan and Yemen.

WHO supports elimination efforts in both countries.
