Sustainable Development Goals

What is SDG?

The Sustainable Development Goals are a set of 17 global targets created by the United Nations to address urgent social economic and environmental challenges around the world. Their purpose is to end poverty improve health and education reduce inequality promote economic growth and take action on climate change while protecting important natural resources like oceans and forests. These goals invite all countries both developed and developing to work together in a global partnership to create a better and more sustainable future for all.

The Role of SDGs in Our Project

Our project is strongly connected to Sustainable Development Goal 3, which aims to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for people at every age. The main motivation for our work is to support this goal by tackling Onchocerciasis, a neglected tropical disease that remains a significant public health issue in many areas. Our focus is on analyzing data to identify regions where Onchocerciasis is more prevalent. Through this approach, we hope to enable early awareness, improve the distribution of resources, and support informed decisions—ultimately helping to enhance health and well-being in the communities affected by this disease.

SDG 3.3

By 2030, the goal is to stop the spread of diseases like AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, neglected tropical diseases, hepatitis, waterborne diseases, and other infections.

Our project is closely linked to this goal, especially target 3.3, because it focuses on Onchocerciasis, a neglected tropical disease that mostly affects rural areas in sub-Saharan Africa. We analyze data to find places with the most cases of the disease. This helps health workers plan better treatments using the medicine ivermectin, use resources more wisely, and keep track of how well the efforts are working. Although we do not provide the treatment ourselves, our work with data supports the global goal to reduce the disease and help end it by 2030.
